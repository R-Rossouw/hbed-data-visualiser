Left vs Right per axledifferential view

Each point is one axle: left-bearing °C (x) against right-bearing °C (y). On the diagonal → both sides equal (healthy). Off the diagonal → one side hotter; the distance from the diagonal is the differential. Points outside the shaded band exceed the 40 °C Type 2 differential limit. A whole cloud shifted off-diagonal one way suggests detector L/R bias, not many faults.

Visualisation aid only. This tool renders measured ITCMS Conditions data and draws the BBF3244 alarm limits for reference (Type 2 absolute 93 °C, Type 3 absolute 120 °C, Type 2 differential 40 °C). It does not raise alarms or determine alarm validity — the controlled specification BBF3244 and the ITCMS govern. “Rise above ambient (ΔT)” is a diagnostic view (bearing − ambient air temperature); the spec alarm limits are absolute and are not applied in that view.
